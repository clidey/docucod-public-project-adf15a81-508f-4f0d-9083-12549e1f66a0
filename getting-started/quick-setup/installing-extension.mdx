# Installing uBO Scope

This guide walks you through the process of installing the uBO Scope browser extension on supported browsers like Chrome, Firefox, and Safari. By following these straightforward steps, you'll quickly add uBO Scope to your browser and begin monitoring all remote server connections attempted by your browser.

---

## 1. Supported Browsers & Version Requirements

uBO Scope supports the following browsers, subject to minimum version requirements:

- **Google Chrome and Chromium-based browsers** (version 122.0 and above)
- **Mozilla Firefox** (version 128.0 and above, including Firefox for Android)
- **Safari** (version 18.5 and above)

Ensure your browser is updated to meet these minimum versions to guarantee compatibility.

For detailed system and permissions requirements, refer to [Prerequisites & System Requirements](https://your-docs-path/getting-started/quick-setup/prerequisites-system-requirements).

---

## 2. Installation Steps by Browser

Follow the steps below depending on your browser of choice.

### 2.1. Installing on Chrome / Chromium-based Browsers

1. **Open the [Chrome Web Store page for uBO Scope](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp).**
2. Click **Add to Chrome**.
3. When prompted, click **Add extension** to confirm.
4. Once installed, the uBO Scope icon will appear in your browser toolbar.

### 2.2. Installing on Mozilla Firefox

1. **Open the [Firefox Add-ons page for uBO Scope](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/).**
2. Click **Add to Firefox**.
3. Review the permissions prompt and click **Add**.
4. Once installed, the extension icon will be visible in your toolbar.

### 2.3. Installing on Safari

1. Update Safari to version 18.5 or later.
2. Visit the App Store and search for uBO Scope or download it from the developer’s distribution if available.
3. Follow Apple's standard extension installation process.
4. Enable uBO Scope in Safari’s Extensions preferences.

> **Note:** Safari requires explicit enabling of extensions post-installation via Preferences.

---

## 3. Verify Installation

After installation, verify uBO Scope is working:

1. Look for the uBO Scope icon in your browser toolbar.
2. Click the icon to open the popup panel.
3. You should see **Zero or more domains connected**—this means uBO Scope is tracking network requests.

If the popup shows 'NO DATA' or does not open, reload your browser and try again.

---

## 4. First Launch & Immediate Use

Once installed, uBO Scope begins monitoring connections immediately without additional configuration.

- Navigate to any website.
- Click the uBO Scope icon to review the number of distinct third-party remote servers contacted.
- Observe three sections reporting domains: **not blocked (allowed), stealth-blocked, and blocked**.

This real-time insight helps you audit your browser’s network activity effortlessly.

---

## 5. Common Pitfalls & Troubleshooting Tips

- **Extension Icon Missing:** Ensure the extension is enabled in your browser's extensions list and the browser has been restarted after installation.

- **No Data in Popup:** Some browsers require reloading the active tabs after installation. Reload open tabs or restart your browser.

- **Version Compatibility:** Verify your browser meets minimum version requirements specified above.

- **Permissions Denied:** Confirm the extension has necessary permissions to access the `webRequest` API.

For extensive troubleshooting, see [Troubleshooting Common Installation Issues](https://your-docs-path/getting-started/setup-and-validation/troubleshooting-common-issues).

---

## 6. Security & Privacy Notes

uBO Scope uses browser permissions to observe network requests but does not modify or block them. It is designed purely for transparency, respecting your privacy.

Always install extensions from official browser stores or trusted sources.
