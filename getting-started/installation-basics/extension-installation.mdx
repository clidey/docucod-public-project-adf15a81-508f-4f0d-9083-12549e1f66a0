# Install uBO Scope

This guide walks you through the straightforward process of installing uBO Scope, a browser extension that reveals all third-party remote server connections during your web browsing. Whether you use Chrome, Firefox, or Safari, follow the specific instructions below to install the extension, verify it’s successfully added, and locate its icon in your browser toolbar.

---

## Supported Browsers

uBO Scope supports the following browsers:

- **Google Chrome** (Minimum version 122.0)
- **Mozilla Firefox** (Minimum version 128.0)
- **Safari** (Minimum version 18.5)


If your browser version is below these minimums, please update your browser before continuing.

---

## Step 1: Install uBO Scope Extension

Choose your browser below and follow the link to the official installation page.

### Google Chrome

1. Open the [Chrome Web Store page for uBO Scope](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp).
2. Click **Add to Chrome**.
3. Confirm the installation by clicking **Add Extension** in the popup.

### Mozilla Firefox

1. Open the [Mozilla Add-ons page for uBO Scope](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/).
2. Click **Add to Firefox**.
3. Confirm any prompts to complete the installation.

### Safari

Safari extensions are distributed via the Mac App Store or through native extension packaging. To install:

1. Open Safari and go to **Safari** > **Settings** > **Extensions**.
2. Look for uBO Scope in the available extensions. If not listed, visit the developer’s page or app store as recommended.
3. Enable uBO Scope by checking the box next to its name.


<Tip>
Make sure your Safari version is 18.5 or newer to ensure compatibility with uBO Scope.
</Tip>

---

## Step 2: Verify Installation

After installation, verify that uBO Scope has been correctly added and is ready to use.

### Locate the Extension Icon

- In **Chrome and Firefox**, uBO Scope's icon resembles a shield with a small counter badge. It appears in the browser toolbar next to the address bar.
- In **Safari**, the icon is accessible from the toolbar or via the extensions sidebar depending on your Safari settings.


### Confirm Functionality

1. Click the uBO Scope icon to open its popup interface.
2. The popup should show 'NO DATA' initially when no website data is loaded.
3. Navigate to any webpage. The icon badge will update to show the count of distinct third-party remote servers connected for that page.


<Check>
If the icon does not appear:
- Ensure the extension is enabled in your browser’s extensions or add-ons settings.
- For Chrome and Firefox, check the browser’s extension management panel.
- For Safari, verify the extension is enabled under Safari > Settings > Extensions.
</Check>

---

## Step 3: Basic Permissions and Requirements

uBO Scope requires the following permissions to operate correctly:

- Access to webRequests: to monitor network connections made by web pages.
- Access to active tabs: to correlate network data with the currently active browser tab.
- Storage permissions: to save session data and the Public Suffix List for domain classification.

The extension operates under Manifest Version 3, ensuring compatibility and enhanced security.


<Note>
During installation, your browser will prompt for these permissions. Confirm to enable full functionality.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting Installation Issues

If you encounter issues during or after installation, try the following:

- **Reload your browser** after installation to ensure the extension initializes properly.
- **Check browser version compatibility** to confirm you meet minimum version requirements.
- **Disable conflicting extensions** that might interfere with webRequest listeners.
- **Restart your computer** if the extension icon does not appear after installation.

For additional help, visit our [Common Installation & Startup Issues](https://example.com/getting-started/troubleshooting/common-setup-issues) guide.
