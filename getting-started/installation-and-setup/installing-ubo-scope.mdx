# Installing uBO Scope

Welcome to your first step toward gaining deep insight into your browser's network connections. This guide walks you through installing uBO Scope, the extension that transparently reports all connections to remote servers your browser attempts or completes. By following these steps, you'll soon see uBO Scope's icon on your browser toolbar, ready to reveal the hidden details of your web traffic.

---

## Supported Browsers and Installation Sources

uBO Scope is available for the following browsers, each with a dedicated installation source:

| Browser    | Installation Source                                                  |
|------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Chromium   | [Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp) |
| Firefox    | [Firefox Add-ons](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/)            |
| Safari     | Available via the Safari Extensions Gallery or Apple App Store (check your Safari browser extension store)                  |


## Step-by-Step Installation Instructions

### 1. Verify Your Browser Compatibility

- Ensure your browser is updated to at least the minimum supported version:
  - Chromium-based browsers: version 122 or later
  - Firefox: version 128 or later
  - Safari: version 18.5 or later


### 2. Install from the Browser Extension Marketplace

Follow the instructions specific to your browser:

<Steps>
<Step title="Install on Chromium-based Browsers (Chrome, Edge, etc.)">
1. Open the [Chrome Web Store page for uBO Scope](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp).
2. Click the **Add to Chrome** button.
3. Confirm any permission prompts that appear.
4. Upon completion, the uBO Scope icon will appear on your browser toolbar.

<Tip>
If you don't see the icon immediately, check browser extensions via `chrome://extensions/` and ensure uBO Scope is enabled.
</Tip>
</Step>
<Step title="Install on Firefox">
1. Visit the [Firefox Add-ons page for uBO Scope](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/).
2. Click **Add to Firefox**.
3. Allow the extension permissions when prompted.
4. uBO Scope’s icon will appear on the toolbar once installed.

<Note>
In Firefox, you may pin the icon to your toolbar by clicking the puzzle-piece menu and selecting the pin next to uBO Scope.
</Note>
</Step>
<Step title="Install on Safari">
1. Open the Safari Extensions Gallery or Apple's App Store.
2. Search for uBO Scope.
3. Install the extension following the standard Safari extension installation prompts.
4. Enable the extension in Safari Preferences under the Extensions tab.

<Warning>
Safari support requires version 18.5 or newer. Older versions may not support the necessary permissions or APIs.
</Warning>
</Step>
</Steps>


### 3. Confirm uBO Scope is Installed

Once installed, locate the uBO Scope icon on your browser toolbar. It should look similar to the extension's logo.

- In some browsers, you may need to restart the browser or refresh open tabs to initialize the extension.
- The icon will dynamically display the count of distinct third-party remote servers accessed by the active tab.

<Check>
If the icon is missing or the count is not visible:
- Verify that the extension is enabled.
- Check that the extension has the required permissions (most importantly, 'webRequest' and 'activeTab').
- Restart the browser and try again.
</Check>

## Post-Installation

Once installed and enabled:

- Browse the web as usual.
- Observe the badge count on the uBO Scope icon update dynamically to reflect the number of distinct remote third-party servers connected by the current tab.
- Click the icon to open the popup UI, where detailed connection outcomes are displayed (allowed, stealth-blocked, blocked).


## Troubleshooting Common Installation Issues

<AccordionGroup title="Common Installation Issues and Solutions">
<Accordion title="Extension Icon Not Visible after Installation">
- Pin the extension to the toolbar via your browser's extensions menu.
- Ensure the extension is enabled in the browser's extensions/add-ons page.
- Restart your browser to complete activation.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Installation Button Grayed Out or Disabled">
- Check that your browser version meets the minimum requirements.
- Clear your browser cache or try installing in a fresh browser profile.
- Verify network connectivity and that the extension store is reachable.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Permissions Not Granted Promptly">
- Extensions require permissions such as access to tabs and webRequest data.
- Accept all permission prompts during installation to ensure functionality.
- If denied, disable and re-enable the extension to trigger permissions.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
