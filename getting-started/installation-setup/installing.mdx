# Installing uBO Scope

Welcome to the installation guide for uBO Scope. This page provides **clear, step-by-step instructions** to quickly download, install, and activate the uBO Scope browser extension on your preferred major browser — Chrome, Firefox, or Safari. Within minutes, you can have uBO Scope monitoring and reporting third-party network connections for enhanced privacy insights.

---

## Supported Browsers and Versions

Before starting, ensure your browser meets these minimum version requirements:

- **Google Chrome / Chromium-based browsers**: version 122.0 or higher
- **Mozilla Firefox**: version 128.0 or higher
- **Apple Safari**: version 18.5 or higher

If you are unsure about your browser version, check your browser's About page and update if needed.

<Check>
Outdated browsers may prevent the extension from functioning correctly. Updating to the latest stable version is critical.
</Check>

---

## Installation Steps by Browser

### 1. Google Chrome / Chromium-Based Browsers

#### Step 1: Open the Chrome Web Store
- Navigate to the [uBO Scope extension page in the Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp).

#### Step 2: Add Extension
- Click the **Add to Chrome** button.
- Confirm any prompts that ask for permissions.

#### Step 3: Verify Installation
- The uBO Scope icon (shaped like a small shield) should appear in your browser toolbar.

### 2. Mozilla Firefox

#### Step 1: Open Firefox Add-ons
- Visit the [uBO Scope extension page on Firefox Add-ons](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/).

#### Step 2: Install Extension
- Click **Add to Firefox**.
- Approve all permission requests.

#### Step 3: Confirmation
- The uBO Scope icon now appears in the Firefox toolbar.

### 3. Apple Safari

#### Step 1: Access Safari Extensions
- Open Safari and go to the Safari Extensions section (found via Safari → Settings → Extensions or the App Store).

#### Step 2: Install uBO Scope
- Find uBO Scope by searching the store.
- Click **Install**.

#### Step 3: Enable Extension
- In Safari settings under Extensions, ensure uBO Scope is **checked to enable**.

#### Step 4: Confirm the Icon
- You should see the uBO Scope icon in Safari’s toolbar.

## Immediate Post-Installation

### Activate the Extension
- For some browsers, after installation, you may need to manually enable uBO Scope in your extensions or add-ons manager.

### Understand Permissions
- The extension requires permissions to access active tabs, storage, and the ability to monitor network requests. These are essential for it to accurately record third-party connections.

### Restart Browser (if needed)
- If the extension does not appear immediately, restart your browser and verify again.

---

## Verification and First Use

### Check Toolbar Icon and Badge
- The uBO Scope icon displays a badge showing the number of distinct third-party servers contacted by the current active tab.
- A lower badge count usually indicates fewer tracking or external connections.

<Tip>
Click the uBO Scope icon to open the popup UI and view detailed categorized connection data (allowed, stealth-blocked, and blocked).
</Tip>

### Open and Explore
1. Click the extension icon in your browser toolbar.
2. The popup displays summary data of domains connected during current browsing.
3. Confirm the extension is actively monitoring by visiting any website and watching the badge update.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup title="Installation Troubleshooting">
<Accordion title="Extension Icon Does Not Appear">
- Ensure your browser version is supported.
- Check if the extension is disabled in your browser’s extensions or add-ons manager.
- Restart your browser if the icon fails to show.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Permissions Not Granted or Blocked">
- uBO Scope requires webRequest permissions and access to tabs for functionality.
- Check your browser prompts during installation and manually enable permissions if blocked.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Browser Not Supported or Version Too Low">
- Update to the latest browser version as older versions lack necessary APIs.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
