## Why Ad Block Test Sites Are Misleading

“Ad block test” websites claim to evaluate your content blockers by simulating network requests designed to detect if ads or trackers are blocked. However, these tests are unreliable and can be misleading for several reasons:

- **Stealth Blocking Techniques**: Modern content blockers, including many extensions, use stealth techniques that hide blocking actions from such test scripts to avoid detection and prevent breakage of websites. Ad block tests can never "see" these stealth actions.

- **Fabricated Network Requests**: The network requests these test sites use are often contrived — these URLs and domains do not exist in real-world browsing scenarios. Passing or failing these tests says nothing about blocking effectiveness on actual websites.

- **False Sense of Security**: These tests give users a false sense of confidence or failure based purely on artificial scenarios rather than genuine blocking.

<u>Result:</u> Relying on these test sites wastes time and can mislead your privacy and content blocking choices.

---

## The Block Count Myth

A common misconception is that a higher block count badge on a content blocker extension means better protection. This belief is flawed because:

- **Badge Counts Represent Blocked Requests, Not Servers**: The badge number is a raw count of blocked network requests, not the number of distinct remote servers blocked.

- **Higher Block Count Can Mean More Allowed Connections**: Ironically, a blocker with a high block count may allow more distinct third-party connections, indicated by a higher count of unique allowed domains. This means more potential privacy exposure.

- **Quality Over Quantity**: Effective blocking reduces allowed third-party servers to a minimum, focusing on blocking at the source rather than just increasing the total block requests.

## Using uBO Scope Effectively to Debunk These Myths

uBO Scope provides an independent, transparent view of what remote servers your browser connects to — regardless of what content blocker you're using. With uBO Scope, you can:

- **See All Connections Clearly**: The popup shows domains categorized as allowed, stealth-blocked, or blocked.

- **Assess Privacy Realistically**: Focus on reducing *allowed domain counts*, not just block counts.

- **Spot Stealth Blocking**: Detect when requests are silently blocked or redirected without increasing the block count.

- **Ignore Badge Noise**: Evaluate content blocker impact based on the actual domains your browser communicates with today.

## Step-by-Step Workflow to Challenge Misleading Metrics

<Steps>
<Step title="Open uBO Scope for the Active Tab">
Click the uBO Scope toolbar icon to open the popup. Ensure the page you want to analyze is active.
</Step>
<Step title="Review the Domain Counts">
Look at the summary ‘domains connected’ number and the breakdown into “not blocked,” “stealth-blocked,” and “blocked” sections.
</Step>
<Step title="Compare Counts to Blocker Badge Counts">
Notice the number of allowed domains vs. the content blocker's badge count. A lower allowed domains count means less third-party exposure.
</Step>
<Step title="Ignore Ad Block Test Site Results">
If analyzing an ad block test site, understand the results don't represent real-world blocking. Focus on how many real domains are contacted, not the badge count or test pass/fail.
</Step>
<Step title="Make Privacy Decisions Based on Allowed Domains">
Prioritize reducing allowed domains in uBO Scope over increasing block counts. Less allowed domains means fewer third-parties tracking or loading resources.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Real-World Example

Suppose you visit an "ad block test" website that reports your blocker fails because it shows 20 blocked requests on their own test domains.

- Open uBO Scope.
- Notice the number of **allowed domains** is still high—say 15 domains—which means your browser connects to 15 distinct third-party servers unrelated to the test.

This tells you your blocker may block the test domains but still allows many real third-party connections.

Conversely, if another blocker shows a higher block count badge but also reports 25 allowed distinct domains in uBO Scope, it may be less effective overall.

## Best Practices & Tips

- Always use uBO Scope's popup to assess privacy exposure on real websites.
- Use allowed domain counts as your main metric—not block counts or ad block test pass statuses.
- Ignore "ad block test" websites as benchmarking tools.
- Learn stealth-blocked domains in the popup to grasp subtle block behavior.
- Combine uBO Scope’s insights with your content blocker’s filter settings for better control.

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Ad Block Test Site Shows Unexpected Results">

- Understand these sites do not emulate real web conditions.
- Use uBO Scope on typical websites instead to observe realistic connection data.
