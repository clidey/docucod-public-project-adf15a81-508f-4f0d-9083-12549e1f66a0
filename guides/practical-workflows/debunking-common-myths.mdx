# Debunking Content Blocking Myths with Real Data

Discover how to critically assess the effectiveness of content blockers using uBO Scope’s real-time and accurate network connection data. This guide will equip you to see beyond misleading badge counts and unreliable ‘ad blocker test’ webpages by providing practical methods and insights based on uBO Scope's metrics.

---

## 1. Understanding What uBO Scope Measures

uBO Scope reveals **all remote server connections** your browser attempts or completes, independent of any content blocker in use. Its key metric is the **toolbar badge count**, which reflects the number of **distinct third-party domains from which resources were successfully loaded**, not just blocked requests.

### Why Does This Matter?

- Most content blockers report how many requests they block, but this metric alone is misleading.
- A **higher block count does not equal better blocking**; it could mean more connections were attempted.
- The more important figure is the number of unique third parties from which the browser received content — the lower this number, the better your privacy and blocker effectiveness.

<Tip>
Always trust **uBO Scope's badge count for allowed connections** as the fundamental indicator of actual third-party exposure.
</Tip>

---

## 2. Myths That uBO Scope Helps Debunk

### Myth 1: "The content blocker with the highest block count is the best"

This misconception overlooks that those blockers might allow more distinct third-party connections despite blocking more requests. uBO Scope shows the real exposure by counting the **allowed unique third-party domains**.

### Myth 2: "Ad blocker test webpages represent real-world blocking effectiveness"

- Such test pages often create **unrealistic or contrived network requests** that don't reflect typical browsing behavior.
- Many content blockers employ **stealth techniques** to avoid revealing their blocking in such tests.
- uBO Scope exposes actual network request outcomes during normal browsing, providing an unfiltered view.

<Warning>
Avoid relying on 'ad blocker test' webpages as benchmarks—they do not simulate real web environments or blocker behavior.
</Warning>

## 3. How to Use uBO Scope to Evaluate Content Blockers

Follow this simple workflow to make informed assessments:

### Prerequisites
- Install and enable uBO Scope on your browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari).
- Have your content blockers enabled one at a time for clear comparison.
- Choose a consistent testing website for evaluation.

### Step-by-Step Comparison

<Steps>
<Step title="Open uBO Scope">
Click the uBO Scope toolbar icon to open the popup and observe the badge count.
</Step>
<Step title="Browse a test website">
Load your chosen website and wait for the badge count and popup to update.
</Step>
<Step title="Note allowed domains count">
Record the number of unique allowed third-party domains shown in the badge and popup’s “not blocked” section.
</Step>
<Step title="Toggle content blockers">
Disable the current blocker and enable another, then repeat the browsing and observation.
</Step>
<Step title="Compare results across blockers">
A lower number of allowed third-party domains means fewer external connections were made, indicating better blocking effectiveness.
</Step>
</Steps>

### What You Should See
- The **badge updates in real-time**, reflecting accurate connection counts.
- The **popup categorizes domains by allowed, stealth-blocked, and blocked** outcomes.
- Comparing allowed counts across blockers highlights the true privacy protection level.

---

## 4. Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

- **Do not confuse blocked request counts with actual exposure**; focus on the allowed third-party domain count.
- Beware of **browser or network-level DNS blocking**, which uBO Scope cannot always detect if outside `webRequest` API scope.
- **Test on real browsing sessions**, not only synthetic test pages.

<Note>
uBO Scope works best alongside your content blockers, revealing their real impact on external connections.
</Note>

---

## 5. Best Practices for Accurate Evaluations

- Use websites you regularly visit as test cases rather than artificial test pages.
- Enable only one content blocker at a time to isolate effects.
- Refresh pages and monitor uBO Scope badge and popup during normal browsing.
- Consider a lower badge count the goal for privacy-focused blocking.

---

## 6. Practical Scenario Illustration

Imagine you want to assess whether Blocker A or Blocker B is more effective on a news site:
- With Blocker A active, uBO Scope’s badge shows **4 allowed domains**.
- With Blocker B active, the badge shows **7 allowed domains**.

Despite Blocker B potentially blocking more requests, the data proves Blocker A is revealing fewer third-party connections — the better choice for your privacy.

## 8. Troubleshooting

### Problem: Badge count not updating
- Verify permissions are correctly granted to uBO Scope.
- Restart the browser and reload the extension.

### Problem: Expected blocked domains show as allowed
- Remember some may be stealth-blocked or handled outside browser control.
- Confirm content blocker is enabled and configured correctly.

### Tip
Keep uBO Scope updated to leverage the latest fixes and features for precise metrics.
