# Debunking Ad Blocker Test Webpages

## Overview

This guide explores why traditional "ad blocker test" webpages frequently mislead users when evaluating content blockers and demonstrates how uBO Scope provides a reliable, real-world method to validate blocking effectiveness. Rather than relying on synthetic, fabricated tests, uBO Scope reveals actual network activity in your browser, giving you unbiased insight into third-party connections—including those allowed, blocked, or stealth-blocked.

---

## Why Ad Blocker Test Pages Mislead

"Ad blocker test" webpages often create an illusion of blocking success or failure based on behaviors that do not reflect real-world browsing:

- **Artificial Network Requests**: These test pages create unusual or single-purpose network calls that do not represent everyday site requests, confusing blockers.
- **Detection Evasion**: Content blockers may stealthily block some resources to avoid breakage or detection, actions which these tests can't detect.
- **Superficial Metrics**: Block counts shown on these pages often don't reflect the real number of distinct third-party servers contacted.

<Tip>
Avoid trusting "ad blocker test" webpages to assess your privacy tools. Their scenarios are contrived and don't represent genuine web interactions.
</Tip>

---

## How uBO Scope Provides Real-World Validation

uBO Scope tracks all network connections your browser attempts on a per-tab basis, regardless of which content blocker you use. It does so by monitoring actual network events via the browser's webRequest API and categorizing every third-party domain as:

- **Allowed**: Domains your browser successfully connected to.
- **Blocked**: Domains where requests failed or were blocked.
- **Stealth-blocked**: Domains where redirects or subtle blocking occurred.

This categorization reflects true, practical blocking effectiveness, offering clarity beyond surface block counts or test scenarios.

Follow these steps to confidently evaluate your content blocker using uBO Scope instead of relying on ad blocker test pages.

<Steps>

</Steps>

You’ll identify the actual number of distinct third-party remote servers your browser connects to, filtered accurately by your content blocker settings.

Lower counts of allowed third-party domains indicate more effective blocking in real-world conditions.

## Real-World Example

Suppose you run a popular social media platform, and its page loads several resources:

- **Allowed**: cdn.social.com (a CDN delivering images and scripts)
- **Stealth-blocked**: trackers.adservice.com (requests redirected silently)
- **Blocked**: ads.network.com (fully blocked ad network)

In this scenario, uBO Scope reveals the subtle stealth blocking invisible to test pages, while also showing which domains truly load content.

This real network insight helps you assess whether the content blocker is doing what it promises in real browsing behavior.

## Best Practices

- **Use uBO Scope for real browsing analysis:** Conduct tests on actual websites you use, not synthetic test pages.
- **Understand domain categorization:** Know the difference between allowed, stealth-blocked, and blocked for better decision-making.
- **Combine with filter list maintenance:** Use insights from uBO Scope to fine-tune your filter lists or choose blockers.
- **Regularly validate blocker behavior:** Network conditions and domains evolve; periodic reviews keep your blocking effective.

---

## Common Pitfalls

- **Misinterpreting badge counts:** The badge indicates distinct allowed third-party domains, not total blocked requests.
- **Relying on test pages:** Fake network requests in test pages do not reflect real web behavior and can produce misleading results.
- **Ignoring stealth blocking:** Some blockers quietly redirect requests; uBO Scope surfaces this, but many tests don’t.

<Warning>
Relying on block count alone or on artificial test sites can lead to wrong conclusions about your privacy setup.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

If uBO Scope shows no data or odd results:

- Confirm uBO Scope is installed and enabled properly.
- Verify your browser supports the webRequest API (check [Prerequisites & System Requirements](/getting-started/setup-and-installation/prerequisites-system-requirements)).
- Check for permission prompts and allow uBO Scope to monitor network requests.
- Ensure you are browsing real, active web pages rather than local or internal browser pages.

For installation or first-run issues, see [Troubleshooting Installation & First-Run Issues](/getting-started/troubleshooting/common-installation-issues).
