# Debunking Ad Blocker Myths with uBO Scope

Discover how uBO Scope provides clarity around common misconceptions about ad blockers by exposing the realities behind block counts and ad blocker test pages. This guide walks you through realistic scenarios using uBO Scope's detailed network insights to fact-check widely held myths, helping you understand what truly impacts your online privacy.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Understand What the Toolbar Badge Count Represents

- The number displayed in the uBO Scope toolbar badge reflects the count of **distinct third-party domains** your browser successfully connected to on the active tab.
- This number is not a "block count" but an indication of how many unique third-party origins your browser fetched resources from, directly affecting your privacy footprint.

<Tip>
Never equate a higher block count number from a content blocker to better privacy or more effective blocking. A high block count can misleadingly correlate with allowing connections to numerous distinct third-party hosts, which implies less actual blocking.
</Tip>

#### How to Verify
- Open the popup on an active tab.
- Observe the “domains connected” count and the separated lists:
  - **Not blocked**: Domains your browser connected to.
  - **Stealth-blocked**: Domains blocked silently to avoid detection.
  - **Blocked**: Domains that failed to load due to blocking.


### 2. Debunking the "Higher Block Count = Better" Myth

- Commonly, users believe that if one blocker reports more blocked items than another, it is superior.
- uBO Scope shows that a content blocker with a higher block count may actually fetch from **more third-party domains**, which increases privacy risk.

#### Practical Scenario
- Compare block counts of two content blockers on the same website.
- Use uBO Scope to check the badge count and popup domains:
  - A lower badge number means fewer distinct third-party connections.
  - Verify whether the domains reported as allowed are fewer in number.

<Tip>
Assess blocker effectiveness by focusing on how many unique third-party domains are allowed, not by tallying blocked items.
</Tip>

### 3. Spot the Limits of Online "Ad Blocker Test" Webpages

- These test pages generate artificial network requests that real-world websites do not use.
- They are unable to account for stealth blocking techniques utilized by modern extensions.
- Consequently, test results may falsely label a blocker as ineffective or fail to detect its presence.

#### Verifying with uBO Scope
- Visit an ad blocker test website.
- Open the uBO Scope popup and observe actual domains connected.
- Notice discrepancies between test page messages and actual network connections.

<Tip>
Avoid basing your content blocker choice or configuration decisions on such test pages—they don’t represent real traffic.
</Tip>

### 4. Use uBO Scope to Monitor Real Privacy Impact

- During normal browsing, open the uBO Scope popup to see which third-party domains your browser connects to.
- Compare these to what you expect to be allowed or blocked.
- Pay attention to stealth domains which may be blocked silently and don't appear as traditional blocks on other tools.

#### Verifying Real-World Connections
- Refresh pages often to observe domain connection changes dynamically.
- Use popup sections (allowed, stealth-blocked, blocked) to analyze behavior.

### 5. For Filter List Maintainers: Assess Filter Effectiveness

- Use the domain lists in the popup to detect domains that repeatedly appear as "allowed" but should be blocked.
- Use this data to improve filter lists, especially on devices where developer tools are limited.

## Troubleshooting & Tips

### Common Issues
- **Popup shows no data after browsing a page:** Ensure uBO Scope has the required permissions and is active.
- **Badge count does not update:** Reload the tab or try visiting different sites; some browsers may restrict webRequest API in private/incognito modes.
- **Confusion over "blocked" vs. "stealth-blocked":** Remember that stealth-blocked means the requests were blocked silently, without triggering standard block notifications.

### Best Practices
- Use uBO Scope as a complement to your content blocker, not a standalone privacy tool.
- Evaluate blocker effectiveness across various real websites instead of synthetic tests.
- Refresh tabs after filter changes to see current blocking state.

### Performance Considerations
- uBO Scope relies on the browser `webRequest` API; performance depends on browser support and configuration.
- Avoid using on low-resource devices for heavy traffic monitoring.

### Alternative Approaches
- Use uBO Scope to assist filter list maintainers in identifying domains to block.
- Combine uBO Scope insights with classic content blocker logs and privacy reports.
