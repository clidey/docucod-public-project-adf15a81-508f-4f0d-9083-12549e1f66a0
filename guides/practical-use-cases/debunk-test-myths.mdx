# Debunking 'Ad Blocker Test' Webpages with uBO Scope

## Overview
This guide demonstrates how uBO Scope exposes the limitations and unreliability of so-called 'ad blocker test' webpages. Many users rely on these test pages to measure how well their content blocker is working, but these sites often provide misleading results by simulating unrealistic network requests and failing to detect stealth blocking techniques commonly used by modern content blockers.

Understanding these limitations is crucial for anyone who wants accurate insights into the effectiveness of their content blocker. uBO Scope provides transparency by reporting actual remote server connections, allowing you to see real network activity and bypass misleading test page techniques.

---

## Why 'Ad Blocker Test' Webpages Are Untrustworthy

### 1. Unrealistic Network Requests
Ad blocker test sites often use fabricated domains and unusual requests that do not represent real-world browsing. Because these domains and requests are artificial, the results do not correlate with genuine blocking effectiveness.

### 2. Stealth Blocking Techniques
Content blockers like uBlock Origin often use stealthy blocking to prevent breakage or detection. These techniques do not appear as conventional blocked requests in the browser. Many test sites cannot detect stealth blocking and therefore give false impressions of blocker failure.

### 3. Misleading Metrics
Test pages frequently equate block counts or loaded resource counts with effectiveness. However, what truly matters is how many distinct third-party domains are contacted when loading actual websites—something test pages fail to measure accurately.

---

## How uBO Scope Shows the Real Picture

uBO Scope records and reports all network connection attempts made by webpages, regardless of blocking method. It uses browser WebRequest APIs to log:

- **Allowed Connections:** Third-party domains from which resources actually loaded.
- **Stealth-Blocked Connections:** Requests that were stealthily blocked (not visible to webpages).
- **Blocked Connections:** Requests explicitly blocked by content blockers or network errors.

By displaying these outcomes per domain, uBO Scope gives you a granular and truthful view of your browser's network activity.

## Using uBO Scope to Debunk Test Page Myths

### Step 1: Visit an 'Ad Blocker Test' Webpage
Open your browser with uBO Scope installed and navigate to any popular ad blocker test website.

### Step 2: Open the uBO Scope Popup
Click the uBO Scope toolbar icon to open the popup panel. It will display the domains contacted by the test page visit, categorized by allowed, stealth-blocked, and blocked.

### Step 3: Analyze the Domain Listings
Compare the domains listed:

- Many test sites claim blocking failures, yet uBO Scope shows stealth blocking preventing requests.
- Domains from fabricated test page resources often do not match any real-world cases.
- The number of distinct third-party domains contacted is often small, contradicting the test page’s inflated block counts.

### Step 4: Understand the Badge Count
Note the badge count on uBO Scope’s toolbar icon showing the actual number of distinct third-party domains contacted. A low number here typically correlates with effective blocking, regardless of what the test page claims.

### Step 5: Trust Real Site Visits More
For a meaningful test of your content blocker, visit real, mainstream websites with many third-party resources instead of relying on artificial test pages. Use uBO Scope to measure actual connection attempts and blocking success.

## Practical Tips & Best Practices

- **Avoid Relying on Ad Blocker Test Sites:** Discard results from these sites as unreliable benchmarks.

- **Use uBO Scope to See Real Behavior:** Trust connection data from your regular browsing sessions displayed by uBO Scope.

- **Focus on Distinct Domain Count:** More meaningful than total blocked requests; fewer contacted third-parties mean better blocking.

- **Recognize Stealth Blocking:** Some connections may be blocked invisibly, so a lack of 'blocked' entries on test pages doesn’t imply ineffectiveness.

- **Combine Tools for Insight:** Use uBO Scope alongside your content blocker’s native UI to cross-verify blocking results.

## Example: Real-World Scenario Debunking a Test Page
Consider a test page requesting resources from domains like `testadserver.com` and `fakeadcdn.io`. The test page reports "ads loaded" because these requests returned content.

When visiting this test page with uBO Scope enabled:
