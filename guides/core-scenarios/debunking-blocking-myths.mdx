# Debunking Block Count and Content Blocker Myths

_uBO Scope provides a hands-on methodology to understand the true network request outcomes beyond typical block count metrics or misleading ad blocker test pages. This guide walks you through practical scenarios illuminating why badge counts and block indicators can be deceptive, and how to leverage uBO Scope for accurate assessment of any content blocker’s effectiveness._

---

## 1. Understanding the Limitations of Badge and Block Counts

### Why Block Counts Can Be Misleading

- **Badge counts reflect distinct third-party domains that delivered content, not just blocked requests.** A high block count on other blockers might ironically mean more connections were allowed, as these blockers can unleash a larger variety of third-party resources.
- **Block counts are numeric without context.** They do not differentiate between trivial connections, critical trackers, or stealth-blocked (hidden from webpages) content.

### The Myth: "Higher block count means better blocking"

This misconception falsely equates quantity with quality. uBO Scope reveals that the real privacy impact is the number of distinct third-party domains actually contacted successfully—not just how many requests were blocked.

<Tip>
The number of unique third-party domains allowed through directly influences your exposure. Focus on minimizing allowed third parties rather than maximizing block counts.
</Tip>

### Why Ad Blocker Test Pages are Unreliable

- Many rely on contrived URLs and network requests that **do not reflect real-world browsing behaviors or popular domains.**
- Extension-based blockers often use stealth techniques, making their operation **invisible or different from what such test pages expect.**
- These tests can be exploited by sites to fingerprint or detect blockers, leading to biased or inaccurate results.

### The Myth: "Ad blocker test pages show reliable effectiveness"

These pages fail to represent real content and network flows, leading to skewed evaluations of blockers. uBO Scope instead directly captures real network events as the browser reports them via the `webRequest` API, offering truthful insights.

<Warning>
Do not rely on ad blocker test pages or raw block count badges to judge content blocker reliability or coverage.
</Warning>

## 2. Leveraging uBO Scope to See the Real Picture

### What uBO Scope Reports

- **Allowed Domains:** Third-party domains from which content was successfully fetched.
- **Stealth-Blocked Domains:** Connections that were blocked silently without notifying the webpage.
- **Blocked Domains:** Requests that failed due to blocking (errors).

Unlike other metrics, uBO Scope aggregates connection outcomes directly tied to the browser’s network activity.

### Workflow: Evaluating Content Blockers with uBO Scope

<Steps>
<Step title="Install uBO Scope">
Install uBO Scope from your browser’s official store. Follow the [Installing uBO Scope](../getting-started/setup-introduction-and-installation/installing-ubo-scope) guide.
</Step>
<Step title="Activate and Navigate to Popup">
Ensure uBO Scope is enabled and click the toolbar icon to open its popup.
</Step>
<Step title="Browse a Website with Your Content Blocker Enabled">
Load your favorite or test website normally with your content blocker active.
</Step>
<Step title="View Domain Summary in uBO Scope Popup">
Examine the lists of allowed, stealth-blocked, and blocked domains for the active tab.
</Step>
<Step title="Compare Blocker Settings or Switch Blockers">
Try different content blockers or configurations and observe changes in uBO Scope’s domain counts.
</Step>
<Step title="Interpret Results for Privacy Impact">
Focus on reducing allowed third-party domains rather than maximizing generic block counts.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
When assessing effectiveness, a lower allowed third-party domain count signifies stronger privacy protection.
</Tip>

## 3. Common Gotchas and Practical Best Practices

### Gotcha 1: Block Count Does Not Equal Privacy Gain

- Block counts might reflect only raw network requests blocked, not whether allowed requests enable tracking.
- Focusing solely on block counts may overlook stealth-blocked or still-exposed third parties.

### Gotcha 2: Stealth Blocking Is Invisible to Test Pages

- uBO Scope reveals stealth-blocked domains that are completely hidden from webpages, which test pages cannot observe.

### Gotcha 3: Blocker Detection on Test Pages

- Some test pages actively seek to detect blockers, leading to false positive or negative results for blocker performance.

### Best Practices

- Use uBO Scope alongside your content blockers to get a comprehensive network transparency overview.
- Regularly check the popup domain summary for the sites you visit most to detect unexpected third-party loading.
- Avoid evaluating blockers solely on web-based ad-blocker testing services.

## 4. Hands-On Example: Evaluating YouTube Connections

1. Open your browser with uBO Scope installed and your preferred content blocker active.
2. Navigate to youtube.com.
3. Click the uBO Scope toolbar icon to open the popup.
4. Observe:
   - How many third-party domains are allowed?
   - How many are stealth-blocked or blocked?
5. Temporarily disable your content blocker and reload YouTube.
6. Re-open uBO Scope popup and note the increase in allowed domains.
7. This contrast highlights your content blocker's effectivity beyond mere block counts.

<Note>
This process demonstrates how uBO Scope exposes the real breadth of network connections induced by sites like YouTube.
</Note>

## 5. Troubleshooting Tips

### Issue: Badge Count Discrepancies

- Ensure your browser supports `webRequest` API properly as uBO Scope depends on it.
- Confirm your content blocker and uBO Scope have not conflicting permissions.
- Reload the tab; some network events may take a moment to register.

### Issue: Empty Domain Data in Popup

- Verify you have active network connections (reload the page).
- Check if uBO Scope is enabled and permissions are granted.
- Reset session data via the extension or browser storage.

Refer to the [Basic Troubleshooting](../getting-started/quick-start-usage-validation/basic-troubleshooting) guide for detailed fixes.
