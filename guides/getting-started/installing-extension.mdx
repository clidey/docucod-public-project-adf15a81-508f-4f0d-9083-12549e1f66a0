# Installing uBO Scope

## Overview
This guide helps you quickly set up uBO Scope on your preferred browser. You'll find step-by-step instructions to install the extension, platform-specific details, and permissions required for optimal operation. By the end, you'll have uBO Scope running and ready to provide real-time visibility into third-party network connections for your browsing activity.

---

## Prerequisites
Before installing uBO Scope, ensure you have the following:

- A supported browser:
  - **Google Chrome** (version 122.0 or later)
  - **Mozilla Firefox** (version 128.0 or later)
  - **Safari** (version 18.5 or later)
- A stable internet connection to download the extension

> **Note:** uBO Scope requires the browser to support the `webRequest` API, which is essential for capturing network events accurately.

---

## Installation Steps

### 1. Choose Your Browser and Access the Official Store

- **Google Chrome:**
  - Go to the [Chrome Web Store page for uBO Scope](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/ubo-scope/bbdpgcaljkaaigfcomhidmneffjjjfgp).
- **Mozilla Firefox:**
  - Visit the [Firefox Add-ons page for uBO Scope](https://addons.mozilla.org/firefox/addon/ubo-scope/).
- **Safari:**
  - If available, acquire uBO Scope from the official Safari Extensions Gallery or Apple App Store.

### 2. Install the Extension

- Click the **Add to Browser** or **Install** button.
- Confirm any permission prompts shown by the browser. These permissions include:
  - Access to active tabs (`activeTab`),
  - Storage to save session information (`storage`),
  - Access to web requests to monitor network activity (`webRequest`).

### 3. Confirm Installation

- After installation, look for the uBO Scope icon in your browser's toolbar.
  - If the icon is not visible, check your browser’s extensions or add-ons menu.

### 4. Pin the Extension (Recommended)

- For easy access and consistent badge updates, pin uBO Scope to your toolbar:
  - **Chrome:** Click the puzzle piece icon, find uBO Scope, and click the pin icon.
  - **Firefox:** Use the add-ons menu, drag the icon to the toolbar or use the “Manage Extensions” options.
  - **Safari:** Follow Apple’s instructions to show the extension icon in the toolbar.

### 5. Verify Badge and Permissions

- Once active, the toolbar icon will display a badge showing the count of distinct third-party domains connected on the active tab.
- Ensure that the badge appears and updates as you browse.

---

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Chromium-Based Browsers
- Requires Chrome 122.0 or newer.
- Supports HTTP, HTTPS, WS, and WSS network schemes.
- Uses a service worker as the background process.

### Firefox
- Minimum Firefox version 128.0.
- Supports similar network schemes and background scripts.
- Identified by its unique extension ID: `uBO-Scope@raymondhill.net`.

### Safari
- Requires Safari version 18.5+.
- Supports HTTP and HTTPS but may have limitations on WebSocket monitoring.
- Uses background scripts like Firefox.

---

## First-Run Tips

- After installation and activation, visit a few websites to see uBO Scope in action.
- Click the toolbar icon to open the popup panel and inspect the network request data.
- The badge number reflects distinct third-party domains your browser contacted on the active tab.

<Tip>
A lower badge count generally indicates fewer third-party connections, which can mean improved privacy and reduced exposure.
</Tip>

---

## Verification

1. Open a new browser tab and navigate to your favorite website.
2. Observe the badge on the uBO Scope icon for a number greater than zero.
3. Click the icon to open the popup and review the listed domains under "not blocked", "stealth-blocked", and "blocked" categories.

If these behaviors occur, the installation was successful.

## Troubleshooting Common Installation Issues

<AccordionGroup title="Troubleshooting Installation Problems">
<Accordion title="Extension Icon Not Visible">
Check if the extension is enabled in your browser’s extension settings. Pin it to the toolbar for easier access.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Badge Count Not Updating">
Ensure you've granted all permissions, especially the `webRequest` permission. Sometimes a browser restart can resolve permission application issues.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Installation Fails or Is Blocked">
Verify your browser version meets minimum requirements. Also, confirm you are installing from the official store to avoid corrupted or unofficial builds.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
